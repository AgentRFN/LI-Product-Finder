Common questions
What you need to know
What is the difference between traditional insurance and VUL? +
Traditional insurance (e.g. term, whole life, endowment) provides guaranteed benefits — coverage amount and terms are fixed. VUL (Variable Unit-Linked) combines life insurance with an investment fund. The fund value can grow over time but is subject to market performance and is not guaranteed. Both are valid — the right choice depends on your goals and risk tolerance.
What is the correct order of financial priorities? +
Following the foundational framework: (1) Emergency Fund — 3 to 6 months of living expenses in a liquid account; (2) Protection Insurance — life, accident, health; (3) Savings and Investments — education fund, retirement, wealth building. Insurance protects what you've built. Investment grows what you save. Neither replaces the other.
How soon can I get covered after applying? +
Coverage can begin as early as the day your application is approved, subject to underwriting. Your advisor will guide you through requirements. Some products like PRU Personal Accident and PRUWealth 10 have simplified or guaranteed issue offers with minimal medical requirements.
What happens if I miss a premium payment? +
Most plans have a grace period (typically 31 days) for late payments. VUL plans can also use the accumulated fund value to cover charges during temporary financial difficulty, subject to maintaining a minimum fund value. Traditional plans have non-forfeiture options such as automatic premium loans or reduced paid-up values.
Can I attach riders to my policy? +
Yes — most PRULink VUL products and traditional plans accept optional riders including: Critical Illness (Life Care Benefit), Hospitalization (Hospital Income, ICU, Surgical Expense Reimbursement), Accident (ADD, ATPD, PA riders), and Waiver of Premium. Riders increase the total premium but significantly expand your coverage.
